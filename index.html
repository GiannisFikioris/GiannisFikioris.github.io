About me

I am a second year PhD student in the theory group at Cornell University, advised by Éva Tardos. I received my BSE from the department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, National Technical University of Athens. I completed my undergraduate thesis with Dimitris Fotakis, titled Perturbation Stability for Mechanism Design.

From July 2019 to August 2020, I was working at NCSR “Demokritos” in the Complex Event Recognition Group, advised by Alexander Artikis.

How to pronounce my name.

 Interests. Theoretical Computer Science – Algorithmic Game Theory, Learning Theory.

Work Experience

  • July ‘19 to August ‘20

    Worked as a Research Associate in the Complex Event Recognition Group of NCSR “Demokritos”. I worked on an algorithm that compresses the route of a ship, to make a simpler trajectory with less points. This allows a complex event recognition engine (RTEC) to identify various events more quickly and accurate.
